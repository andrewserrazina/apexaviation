SAN MARCOS
Austin Area Flight Training

Flight Simulator Training for San Marcos Pilots

San Marcos Regional Airport (KHYI) serves a vibrant local aviation community midway between Austin and San Antonio. Apex Aviation is about 35 minutes north in Austin — bringing professional simulator-based training within easy reach of San Marcos and Texas State University pilots, opening January 2027.

Serving Pilots from San Marcos and the Surrounding Area

San Marcos sits at a unique geographic crossroads — roughly equidistant between Austin-Bergstrom (KAUS) and San Antonio International — which means pilots based at San Marcos Regional (KHYI) regularly operate in some of the busiest and most complex airspace in Texas. Texas State University's campus adds a significant population of young aviation enthusiasts and student pilots to the mix. Yet San Marcos has historically lacked access to professional simulator-based scenario training, forcing serious pilots to make the full drive to Austin or San Antonio for anything beyond basic flight instruction. Apex Aviation closes that gap, offering premium AATD simulator training in Austin just 35 minutes up I-35.

What San Marcos Pilots Train For at Apex

Instrument Proficiency Check (IPC)

A thorough, scenario-focused IPC that restores genuine instrument currency — not just the minimum sign-off to get legal again.

Biennial Flight Review (BFR)

Satisfy your FAA flight review and come away a sharper pilot — our BFR sessions prioritize real-world judgment over box-checking.

Instrument Rating Training

Accelerate your instrument rating by logging FAA-creditable AATD hours in the simulator to reduce required aircraft time.

Private Pilot Training

For Texas State students and San Marcos residents starting their aviation journey — the sim is the most efficient first step.

Why Sim Training Makes Sense for San Marcos Pilots

Flying the I-35 corridor between Austin and San Antonio means navigating some of the densest GA and commercial traffic in Texas, often under time pressure with rapidly changing weather rolling in from the Gulf. Simulator training at Apex gives San Marcos pilots dedicated practice time for exactly those conditions — complex ATC environments, deteriorating VMC, and the kind of go/no-go decisions that separate good pilots from great ones. Because a 35-minute drive to Austin is far more reliable than waiting for ideal training weather, consistent progression through your training syllabus becomes achievable even during busy semester schedules at Texas State. The FAA's AATD credit rules mean every simulator hour at Apex is an investment that counts toward your certificates and ratings.
