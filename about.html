Why Apex Exists

The checkride is the beginning,
not the goal.

Most flight training is designed to help students meet a regulatory requirement and pass a checkride. Andrew built Apex to go further — whether that's a student pilot earning their first certificate or an instrument-rated pilot who wants to actually fly in IMC rather than just stay legally current.

The Austin GA community has no shortage of pilots who are technically certified but lack the confidence and proficiency to consistently manage real-world scenarios, changing weather, and unexpected emergencies. That gap — between what a checkride proves and what flying actually demands — is what Apex Aviation is built to close.

Every program at Apex is built around scenario-based instruction, honest debrief, and a standard that prioritizes what you're actually capable of over what your logbook says. From first flight to advanced ratings to recurrent proficiency — one ecosystem, one standard.

Before founding Apex Aviation, Andrew operated Andrew Serrazina Aviation — an aviation education business focused on pilot training, virtual ground schools, checkride preparation, and proficiency development. Through that work he built a growing audience of pilots and aviation enthusiasts while generating more than 2.5 million views of aviation education content across social media platforms.

Andrew's view on instrument training is direct: most IFR pilots are not as proficient as they think they are, and most recurrent training is too easy to actually change that. An IPC that doesn't challenge you isn't improving you — it's just updating your logbook.

He chose simulator-first as the Apex model because it's the only environment where deliberate practice is actually possible. In an aircraft, you're constrained by weather, cost, safety margins, and the limits of what a CFI can safely induce. In the AATD, you can pause at the FAF and discuss technique. You can fail the vacuum pump at 800 AGL. You can run the same missed approach until the response is automatic. That's where real learning happens.

What pilots who train with Andrew should expect: honest assessment, scenarios that don't pull their punches, and a debrief that identifies exactly what to work on next. If you came in hoping for validation, you'll leave with something more useful — a clear picture of where you actually stand.

Our Philosophy

Three things we believe
about good training.

Scenarios over maneuvers

A maneuver list teaches you to fly. A scenario teaches you to decide. Every Apex session is built around a realistic situation — not a series of techniques to demonstrate. The debrief is where most of the learning happens.

Honest feedback, always

We don't give reassurance that isn't earned. If your instrument scan has a gap, we'll tell you — specifically, with the data from the session to back it up. Comfortable debrief produces comfortable pilots. That's not what we're building.

Proficiency over currency

Currency means you can legally fly. Proficiency means you should. We care about the second one. Every session is designed to leave you more capable than when you walked in — not just more current.
