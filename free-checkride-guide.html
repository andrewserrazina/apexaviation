What's Inside

Six areas DPEs
always test on.

The guide covers the topics that come up most on private pilot oral exams — organized so you can study each area with confidence.

Airworthiness

ARROW documents, airworthiness directives, MEL/CDL basics, and how to determine if your aircraft is legal to fly.

Regulations

FARs you must know cold — currency requirements, airspace rules, weather minimums, and PIC responsibilities.

Risk Management

PAVE, IMSAFE, 5P framework — how to evaluate risk the way a PIC does, not just recite acronyms.

Weather Decision Making

METARs, TAFs, winds aloft, PIREPs, and how to make go/no-go decisions the DPE will actually respect.

Aircraft Systems

Pitot-static, vacuum, electrical, fuel — how they work, what fails, and how to brief them during your oral.

Airspace

Class A through G, special use airspace, requirements to enter each, and how to navigate airspace decisions out loud.

Why This Guide Works

DPEs aren't testing
what you memorized.

Most applicants spend too much time memorizing facts and not enough time learning how to think like a PIC. They can recite ARROW. They freeze when the DPE asks why it matters for today's flight.

DPEs are evaluating judgment, decision-making, and risk management — not your ability to recall a regulation word for word.

This guide is built around how real oral exams actually go. Each question comes with a model answer that shows you how to reason through it, not just what to say.

Scenario-based, not trivia-based

Questions are framed the way DPEs actually ask them — in context, with real-world stakes attached.

Model answers show the thinking

You see how a confident PIC walks through a question, not just the technically correct bullet points.

Built for the last 30 days

Focused on what actually comes up on checkride day — no filler, no theory-heavy textbook material.
