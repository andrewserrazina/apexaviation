How Apex Trains Private Pilots

At Apex, the simulator is the primary teaching environment — not a supplement. Every maneuver, procedure, and emergency scenario is introduced in our AATD first. You learn without pressure, repeat without burning fuel, and debrief after every pass. By the time we take a skill to the aircraft, you already know what to do. The airplane is where you apply and confirm what the simulator taught you.

This approach produces faster progress, higher retention, and pilots who arrive at the checkride genuinely prepared — not just minimally current.

FAA Requirements for the Private Pilot Certificate

Under FAA Part 61, the private pilot certificate requires a minimum of 40 total flight hours — including 20 hours of dual instruction and 10 hours of supervised solo flight. Those minimums include 3 hours of instrument training, 3 hours of night flying, and 3 hours of cross-country dual. The national average, however, is closer to 60–70 hours. Building in realistic buffer time isn't pessimism — it's what keeps checkride prep from becoming a scramble.
