Why Apex Aviation

Learn it in the sim.

The Team

Built by a pilot
who needed this training.

Andrew Serrazina founded Apex because the training he wanted as an active IFR pilot didn't exist in Austin — focused, scenario-based recurrent work without the student-pilot machinery around it.

Andrew Serrazina

Founder & Chief Instructor

Andrew's view is simple: most instrument pilots are not as current as they think they are, and most recurrent training is too easy to actually change that. He built Apex because he wanted a place in Austin where a working IFR pilot could get an honest assessment, a hard scenario, and a debrief that didn't pull punches — without having to wade through a flight school structured for private pilot students. Every aspect of the Apex curriculum reflects what he would want as the pilot in the left seat.

Currency means you meet the FAA's minimums to act as PIC. Proficiency means you can actually handle what real IMC throws at you. A pilot can be legally current after six approaches in a simulator on a clear day and still be dangerously unprepared for a partial-panel situation in actual conditions. Apex trains for proficiency — currency is a byproduct.
