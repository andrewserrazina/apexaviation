Flight Simulator Training for Round Rock Pilots

Round Rock pilots are 15 minutes from Austin Executive Airport (KEDC) and about 20 minutes from KAUS — and Apex Aviation puts professional simulator training right in your backyard, opening January 2027.

Serving Pilots from Round Rock and the Surrounding Area

Round Rock has grown into one of the most aviation-curious communities in Central Texas, fueled by a tech-savvy population with the income and interest to pursue serious pilot training. Many Round Rock pilots use Austin Executive Airport (KEDC) for local flying — a convenient general aviation hub just 15 minutes south — while others commute to KAUS for instrument work. What's been missing is a dedicated facility for scenario-based simulator training that doesn't require fighting I-35 traffic. Apex Aviation fills that gap with a professional AATD simulator and structured curriculum designed for working adults who want efficient, high-quality instruction.

What Round Rock Pilots Train For at Apex

Instrument Proficiency Check (IPC)

Regain instrument currency with a structured IPC in the sim — no weather cancellations, no aircraft scheduling conflicts.

Biennial Flight Review (BFR)

Complete your FAA-required flight review with a CFII who focuses on real-world decision-making, not just box-checking.

Instrument Rating Training

Log FAA-creditable simulator hours toward your instrument rating without being at the mercy of VFR-only scheduling windows.

Private Pilot Training

Build foundational skills and airmanship in a controlled environment before transitioning to the actual aircraft.

Why Sim Training Makes Sense for Round Rock Pilots

Round Rock's location north of Austin means any trip to a traditional flight school often turns into a half-day affair once you account for the drive, pre-flight, and debrief. Simulator training at Apex compresses that equation significantly — sessions are efficient, structured, and entirely weather-independent, so a Central Texas thunderstorm season won't derail your training timeline. The FAA allows AATD simulator hours to count toward instrument rating requirements, which means time in our sim is time you don't have to pay for in the air. And because our instruction is scenario-based rather than maneuver-repetition, you leave each session with improved judgment, not just improved stick skills.
