Checkride Preparation

Oral exam coaching and ACS-based scenario work, delivered remotely. We go through every area DPEs actually probe — and we don't stop until your answers are solid and your reasoning is yours, not memorized. Covers private pilot, instrument rating, and commercial pilot practical tests.

Common Questions

Program FAQs

All virtual sessions are conducted live via video call with Andrew. For ground school, sessions follow a structured curriculum and run on the weekly schedule (Mon/Wed PPL, Tue IFR, Thu CPL). Checkride prep sessions are scheduled individually and run 60–90 minutes. You'll need a computer or tablet with a stable internet connection — no special software required.

Ground school hours are logged and can be credited toward the aeronautical knowledge requirements for your certificate. Your CFI will sign off your ground training endorsement upon completion. The knowledge test (written exam) itself must still be taken at an FAA-approved testing center.

Virtual checkride prep focuses on the oral exam component — the knowledge, regulations, and decision-making scenarios a DPE will probe. It's available now. When the Austin AATD facility opens in January 2027, we'll add full practical test scenario training in the simulator — flying the actual maneuvers and procedures you'll demonstrate on your checkride, with debrief after each pass.

Yes. Under 14 CFR 61.57(d), an Instrument Proficiency Check can be conducted in an FAA-approved AATD. Our simulator qualifies. The IPC covers all the required tasks — holds, approaches, partial panel, and emergency procedures — and your CFII can sign you off in the sim. No aircraft required.

Start by emailing us. Andrew will ask a few questions about your background, what you were flying before the gap, and what you want to get back to. Based on that, he'll recommend a starting point — which might be a virtual ground school refresher, a sim-based BFR when Austin opens, or both. There's no one-size-fits-all for rusty pilots, and we don't pretend there is.
