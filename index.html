Why Apex Aviation

The checkride is the beginning,
not the goal.

Most training programs are built around certificates. Apex is built around competence. We work with pilots who are already flying — instrument-rated, aircraft owners, professionals on tight schedules — and we ask a harder question than "are you current?" We ask: how well would you actually handle an unexpected hold at minimums, tonight?

Every Apex session answers that question with a real scenario, a structured debrief, and a clear picture of what to work on next.

  • Scenario-based instruction — every session
  • AATD-certified simulator platform
  • Flexible scheduling for working pilots
  • Recurrent training focus — proficiency over currency

The Team

Built by a pilot
who needed this training.

Andrew Serrazina founded Apex because the training he wanted as an active IFR pilot didn't exist in Austin — focused, scenario-based recurrent work without the student-pilot machinery around it.

Andrew Serrazina

Founder & Chief Instructor

Andrew's view is simple: most instrument pilots are not as current as they think they are, and most recurrent training is too easy to actually change that. He built Apex because he wanted a place in Austin where a working IFR pilot could get an honest assessment, a hard scenario, and a debrief that didn't pull punches — without having to wade through a flight school structured for private pilot students. Every aspect of the Apex curriculum reflects what he would want as the pilot in the left seat.

Our Training Philosophy

Every Apex session is built around realistic scenarios — not rote maneuvers. We train the decision-making, situational awareness, and automation management that differentiate a current pilot from a safe one. Currency is the floor. Proficiency is the goal.
