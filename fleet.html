Why Sim Training Works

The argument for
simulator-first.

A real aircraft is constrained in ways that limit deliberate practice. You can't fail the vacuum pump safely in actual IMC. You can't run the same missed approach six times in a row without burning fuel and wearing out a Hobbs meter. You can't program a 200 RVR ceiling on demand.

The AATD removes every one of those constraints. Every session becomes a controlled experiment: you fly a scenario, debrief what happened, and run it again differently. That's the loop that produces real proficiency — not just more hours in the logbook.

  • Train the scenarios you'll never practice in an aircraft
  • Reset and repeat until the response is automatic
  • Debrief after every pass — not just at the end of the flight
  • No weather cancellations or maintenance delays
