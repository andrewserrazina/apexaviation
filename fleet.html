Primary Trainer

Cessna 172 Skyhawk

High-Wing Single Engine · Glass Cockpit

The Cessna 172 Skyhawk is the world's most produced aircraft and the gold standard for primary flight training. Its forgiving handling, excellent visibility, and stable flight characteristics make it the ideal platform for students learning to fly. Our fleet of C172s is equipped with the Garmin G1000 glass cockpit — the same avionics used in modern commercial aircraft — giving students real-world experience from day one.

IFR Trainer

Piper PA-28 Cherokee

Low-Wing Single Engine · Full IFR Panel

The Piper PA-28 Cherokee is our workhorse for instrument and cross-country training. Its low-wing design provides different handling characteristics than the C172, preparing students for a wider variety of aircraft they'll encounter in their careers. Fully IFR certified and equipped with the Garmin GTN 650 touchscreen navigator, it mirrors the cockpit environment of many modern GA and charter aircraft.

Multi-Engine

Piper PA-44 Seminole

Twin Engine · Advanced Avionics

The Piper Seminole is one of the most popular light twin-engine training aircraft in the world, and for good reason — it's docile enough to be a great trainer yet capable enough to simulate real multi-engine operations. With counter-rotating propellers (eliminating the critical engine), it offers a balanced training environment. Equipped with Garmin G500 and dual GTN navigators, the Seminole prepares students for the complex aircraft they'll fly professionally.

Simulator

Full-Motion Flight Simulator

Our on-site FAA-approved Advanced Aviation Training Device (AATD) replicates the Garmin G1000 cockpit environment. Simulator time counts toward instrument rating requirements and lets you practice approaches, holds, and emergencies without the cost of aircraft fuel.

  • FAA-approved AATD — hours count toward IFR
  • Garmin G1000 NXi cockpit replication
  • Real-world weather & airport scenery
  • Only $65/hr — a fraction of aircraft cost
